3.5 introduces conditional distributions for both discrete and continuous random variables.

Discrete Cases

For two discrete random variables, \( X \) and \( Y \), with the ranges \( \{x_{1}​,x_{2}​,\cdots\} \) and \( \{y_{1}​,y_{2}​,\cdots\} \), the conditional pmf of \( Y \) given that \( X \) takes a specific value \( x_{i} \)​ is defined as:

$$p_{Y}(y_{j}|x_{i})=\frac{p(x_{i},y_{j})}{p_{X}(x_{i})}$$

We can also find the marginal pmf of \( Y \) by summing over all possible values of \( X \) using the conditional distribution:

$$p_{Y}(y_{j})=\sum_{i=1}^{\infty}p(x_{i},y_{j})=\sum_{i=1}^{\infty}p_{Y}(y_{j}|x_{i})p_{X}(x_{i})$$

Finally, the conditional cdf is the sum of the conditional pmf values for all \( y_{j} \)​ that are less than or equal to a specific value \( y \):

$$F_{Y}(y|x_{i})=\sum_{j:y_{j}\le y}^{}p_{Y}(y_{j}|x_{i})$$

Example

Let \( X \) be the number of text messages I get from my mom in a day, and let \( Y \) be the total number of text messages I get in a day. Let's imagine that 75% of all my texts are from my mom. Assume that:

$$X∼\text{Pois}\left( \frac{3}{2} \right)$$

Also assume the marginal pmf of \( X \) is:

$$p_{X}(2)=\frac{e^{-1.5}(1.5)^{2}}{2!}$$

Lastly, assume the joint pmf is:

$$p(i,j)=\frac{e^{-2}3^{i}}{i!(j-i)!2^{j}}$$

Now, if I got two texts from my mom so far today, what is the probability that I get at least two more (from anyone), for a total of four texts? First we use the conditional pmf we just learned, and plug in 2 and 4 for \( X \) and \( Y \):

$$p_{Y}(4|2)=\frac{p(2,4)}{p_{X}(2)}$$

We substitute into the joint pmf:

$$p(2,4)=\frac{e^{-2}\times3^{2}}{2!\times(4-2)!\times2^{4}}=\frac{e^{-2}\times9}{2\times2\times16}=\frac{9e^{-2}}{64}$$

Then we find \( p_{X}(2) \) using the definition of a Poisson distribution:

$$p_{X}(2)=\frac{e^{-1.5}\times(1.5)^{2}}{2\times1}=\frac{2.25e^{-1.5}}{2}=1.125e^{-1.5}$$

Finally we put all this back into the conditional pmf:

$$p_{Y}(4|2)=\frac{\frac{9e^{-2}}{64}}{1.125e^{-1.5}}=\frac{9e^{-2}}{64\times1.125e^{-1.5}}=\frac{9e^{-2}}{72e^{-1.5}}=\frac{1}{8}e^{-0.5}\approx0.0758$$

Meaning the probability that I get four texts in total, given that two of them were from my mom already is approximately 7.58%.

Continuous Cases

In the continuous case, we use the joint pdf \( f(x,y) \) and the marginal pdf \( f_{X}​(x) \). As for the conditional pdf, the density of \( Y \) given \( X=x \) is the ratio of the joint density to the marginal density:

$$f_{Y}(y|x)=\frac{f(x,y)}{f_{X}(x)}$$

And for the conditional cdf, we find the probability that \( Y \) is less than or equal to \( y \) given a specific \( x \), we integrate the conditional pdf:

$$F_{Y}(y|x)=\int_{-\infty}^{y}f_{Y}(t|x)dt$$

Additionally, we can recover the marginal pdf of \( Y \) by integrating the conditional pdf weighted by the marginal of \( X \):

$$f_{Y}(y)=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}f_{Y}(y|x)f_{X}(x)dx$$

Probabilities

Imagine we want to find the total probability that \( Y \) falls within a specific range (set \( A \)), but we don't know \( Y \) directly. However, we do know how \( Y \) behaves for every possible value of \( X \). We can do this like so:

$$P(Y\in A)=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}P(Y\in A|X=x)f_{X}(x)dx$$

What about instead of just looking at \( Y \), we are looking at a region \( B \) in a 2D space (where \( X \) and \( Y \) are coordinates). We want to know the probability that a random point \( (X,Y) \) lands inside this shape. This can be computed with:

$$P((X,Y)\in B)=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}P((x,Y)\in B|X=x)f_{X}(x)dx$$
