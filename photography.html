About

I started taking pictures on a cheap Olympus SP-600UZ (shown to the top right) that I got from Goodwill for $20. It's a pretty basic point and shoot but it took pretty nice pictures and something about it really clicked with me.

Not long after I discovered my parent's old Nikon F60 film camera and bought some film and shot on that. I still shoot film sometimes but now I mostly use a Minolta Maxxum 70 and sometimes a Konica U Mini point and shoot for film.

Then I picked up a Sony A100 DSLR from eBay for cheap. I used that for about a year and then upgraded to a Sony NEX 3 (pictured above in the header), however I frequently switch between that and the A100 because I have more lenses for it. I have a few lenses that I use with the NEX:

  • Sony 18-55mm f/3.5-5.6 OSS Zoom Lens
  • Sony 55-210mm f/4.5-6.3 OSS Zoom Lens

I still use the A100 (shown to the bottom right) quite a lot. It has a CCD sensor and I feel it produces more rich colors than the NEX. I also just have more higher quality lenses for it as this cameraâ€™s lens mount is older and thus the lenses are cheaper. Here is what I use with it:

  • Minolta 50mm f/1.7 Prime Lens
  • Tamron 70-300mm Telephoto/Macro Zoom Lens
  • Tokina AT-X 400mm f/5.6 Telephoto Prime Lens
