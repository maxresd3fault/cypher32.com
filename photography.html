About

I started taking pictures on a cheap Olympus SP-600UZ that I got from Goodwill for $20. It was a fairly basic point and shoot camera but it took pretty nice pictures and something about it really clicked with me.

Not long after I discovered my parent's old Nikon F60 film camera and bought some film and shot on that. I still shoot film sometimes but now I mostly use a Minolta Maxxum 70 and sometimes a Konica U Mini point and shoot for film photos.

Then I picked up a Sony A100 DSLR (shown to the lower right) from eBay for cheap. That was my main camera for about a year and then I bought the much smaller Sony NEX 3 (shown to the upper right) mirrorless camera, and for a while I frequently switched between the NEX 3 and the A100 because I have more lenses for the Sony/Minolta A mount. I only have three lenses that I use with the NEX 3:

  • Sony 18-55mm f/3.5-5.6 OSS Zoom Lens
  • Sony 55-210mm f/4.5-6.3 OSS Telephoto Zoom Lens
  • Canon 50mm f/1.8 Prime Lens (with adapter)

Occasionally I still use the A100; it has a CCD sensor and I feel it produces more rich colors than the NEX. After two years of swapping between the NEX 3 and A100 I upgraded to a Sony A900 (shown in the header), a full frame DSLR still using the A mount. Here is a list of my A mount lens collection, these lenses work on my A100, A900, and Minolta film camera:

  • Carl Zeiss 135mm f/1.8 Telephoto Prime Lens
  • Sigma 28-80mm f/3.5 Macro Zoom Lens
  • Minolta 28-100mm f/3.5 Zoom Lens
  • Minolta 50mm f/1.7 Prime Lens
  • Tamron 28-300mm f/3.5 Telephoto Zoom Lens
  • Tamron 70-300mm f/4 Telephoto/Macro Zoom Lens
  • Tokina AT-X 400mm f/5.6 Telephoto Prime Lens
